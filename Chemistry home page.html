Joaquin Rodriguez-Lopez Group

Electrochemistry Undergraduate Researcher

2016-2018 | Champaign - Urbana, IL

Starting sophomore year, I participated in projects exploring properties of graphene on silicon wafer and probing techniques of ultra-microelectrodes through SECM. I have learned from growing graphene on the hydrogen vacuum furnace, transferring onto substrates through wet transfer, producing UME with etched wires and gold nanoparticles, examining the surface with Scannig Electron Microscope, to controlled experiments with Scanning Electrochemical Microscope and analyzing data with OriginPro and Excel.

California Department of Food and Agriculture

Quality Assurance Intern

2014 | Anaheim, CA

At CDFA in Anaheim, over 100 samples of organic produce are shipped in weekly for testing of illegal substances and pesticides. As the high school intern, I prepared samples from fresh produce and performed multiple steps of spiking, centrifugation, water bath, and filtration on batches of samples for GC/MS and LC/MS measurements. I also managed the organic waste, lab hygiene, equipment calibration, and other duties.

Cultural Heritage and Racial Identity Lab

Undergraduate Researcher

2016 | Champaign - Urbana, IL
